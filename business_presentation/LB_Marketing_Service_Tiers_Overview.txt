ABOUT THIS DOCUMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document provides a full breakdown of LB Marketing's four service tiers:
Month 1 Demo, Starter, Growth, and Premium. It is intended for internal use,
client-facing proposals, and onboarding reference.

Each tier builds on the last. Clients begin with the Month 1 Demo — a low-risk,
no-contract engagement designed to demonstrate measurable results before any
ongoing commitment is made. From there, clients move into Starter, Growth, or
Premium based on their goals, competitive landscape, and appetite for growth.

All tiers are month-to-month. There are no lock-in contracts at any level.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A NOTE ON PRICING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Month 1 Demo:   $200 (one-time)
Starter:        $750/month
Growth:         $1,500/month
Premium:        $2,500/month

At the Premium tier, ad spend (Google Ads and/or Meta Ads) is billed separately
and paid directly by the client to Google/Meta. The $2,500/month covers
management, strategy, creative, and optimisation only — not media spend.

Website maintenance is included at all ongoing tiers (Starter and above) where
the client has an existing website and grants CMS access. This covers content
updates only (hours, services, team info, etc.) — not design changes, new page
builds, or full redesigns. Those are scoped and priced separately as needed.

HOW CLIENTS TYPICALLY PROGRESS THROUGH TIERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Month 1:   Demo engagement. We audit, optimise GBP, respond to reviews, and
           run the first review generation campaign. The client receives a
           before/after report at the end of Week 4. They then decide whether
           to continue.

Month 2+:  Most clients enter Starter after Month 1. This maintains the gains
           from Month 1 and keeps the business active, visible, and growing at
           a manageable pace.

Month 2-3: Clients ready for content, SEO expansion, and social media move to
           Growth. This is the most common long-term tier for established
           businesses that want to compete on search.

Month 4+:  Clients ready for paid advertising, full-funnel tracking, and
           maximum market presence move to Premium. This tier is best suited
           for businesses generating enough revenue to justify ad spend and
           looking to dominate their local market.

Clients can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time with no penalties.

TIER 1 — MONTH 1 DEMO
Price: $200 (one-time, no ongoing commitment)
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
The Month 1 Demo is the entry point for every new client. It is designed to
deliver tangible, measurable results within 30 days — before the client commits
to any ongoing retainer. The goal is to earn trust through visible work, not
promises.

This is not a discovery phase or a setup fee. Real deliverables are produced
and results are documented in a formal before/after report at the end of Week 4.

WEEK-BY-WEEK BREAKDOWN
-----------------------

Week 1 — Full Digital Presence Audit
  A comprehensive review of the client's entire online presence including:
  - Google Business Profile (GBP): completeness, categories, photos, services,
    description, hours, and Q&A section
  - Yelp profile: accuracy, photos, categories, and NAP consistency
  - Website: basic SEO health, mobile responsiveness, contact information
  - Review landscape: total count, star rating, response history, and velocity
  - Local keyword rankings: where the business currently ranks for its top
    practice-area + city search terms
  - Competitor comparison: top 4-5 local competitors benchmarked across
    reviews, GBP completion, and map pack presence

  Output: Written audit report delivered by Day 5. Prioritised action list
  included — items ranked by impact and speed of implementation.

Week 2 — GBP Optimisation + Review Response
  Full optimisation of the Google Business Profile:
  - Every field completed: business name, address, phone, website, hours,
    holiday hours, business description (keyword-informed), categories
    (primary and secondary), services section, attributes, and photos
  - All unanswered reviews responded to professionally — both positive and
    negative. Responses are written in the client's voice, not generic.
  - GBP is one of the highest-leverage local SEO assets available. A fully
    optimised profile is the foundation for map pack entry.

Week 3 — Review Generation Campaign + First Content
  - Personalised SMS and/or email review request messages drafted and sent
    to a list of recent clients/customers provided by the business
  - Messages are written per-vertical — tone and context appropriate for
    legal, salon, or home services clients
  - Target: 5–10 new Google reviews in Month 1
  - Review gating is never used (prohibited by Google policy)
  - Yelp is NOT used for review solicitation (against Yelp policy) — treated
    as a citation and visibility asset only
  - First vertical-specific content published (GBP post and/or website update)

Week 4 — Month-End Before/After Report
  A structured report documenting the starting point vs. end of Month 1:
  - GBP profile completion score (before vs. after)
  - Review count and star rating (before vs. after)
  - GBP profile views and search impressions
  - Local keyword ranking baseline for top 5 terms
  - Review response rate (before vs. after)
  - Summary of work completed and recommended next steps

KEY OUTCOMES BY END OF MONTH 1
-------------------------------
  - GBP fully optimised and active
  - All existing reviews responded to
  - 5–10 new Google reviews targeted
  - Local ranking baseline established
  - Client has a clear picture of where they stand and what comes next

WHAT THE CLIENT NEEDS TO PROVIDE
---------------------------------
  - Owner-level GBP access (takes approximately 2 minutes to grant)
  - 20-minute onboarding call in Week 1 to confirm keywords, priorities,
    and reporting preferences
  - A list of recent customer names and contact info for the review campaign
    (Week 3). LB Marketing drafts all messages — the client provides the list.

TERMS
-----
  - $200 one-time fee
  - No contract, no ongoing commitment
  - If the client is not satisfied by end of Week 4, they walk away.
    No penalties, no awkward conversations.

TIER 2 — STARTER
Price: $750/month | Recommended entry point after Month 1
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Starter is the "maintain and build momentum" tier. It is designed for businesses
that have completed Month 1 and want to keep the gains growing without a large
monthly investment. The focus is on consistency — keeping the GBP active, reviews
flowing, and the business visible month over month.

Starter is best understood as a transition tier. Most clients will stay here for
2–4 months before the results are compelling enough to justify moving to Growth.
Clients who are happy with steady, low-effort visibility maintenance may stay at
this tier indefinitely.

WHAT'S INCLUDED
---------------

GBP Management
  - 4 posts per month published directly to the client's Google Business Profile
  - Post types rotate across: service/practice area updates, social proof
    (showcasing client outcomes or reviews), offers or promotions, and
    educational tips relevant to the vertical
  - Post copy is keyword-informed — written to reinforce the client's target
    local search terms naturally (e.g., "immigration lawyer San Francisco")
  - Images sourced or designed in Canva to accompany each post
  - Profile kept current — hours, services, photos, and attributes updated
    as the business changes
  - Posting cadence: approximately weekly, evenly spaced through the month

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Google rewards active profiles. Regular posts signal to
  Google's algorithm that the business is engaged and relevant, which supports
  map pack visibility. Posts also appear directly in the business listing when
  someone searches — a client who sees fresh, relevant content is more likely
  to call than one who sees a stale profile with no activity.

Yelp Management (where applicable)
  - Yelp profile accuracy maintained: business name, address, phone number,
    website URL, categories, hours, and photos kept consistent and current
  - Yelp is treated as a citation and NAP consistency asset — NOT as a review
    growth channel (Yelp's policies prohibit soliciting reviews)
  - Any business-owner responses to Yelp reviews handled as needed

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Yelp still ranks well in Google search results for local
  queries, particularly for legal and home services. An inconsistent or
  incomplete Yelp profile creates conflicting signals that can suppress local
  search rankings.

Review Management
  - Monthly review request campaign (drip model): personalised SMS and/or email
    messages sent to new clients/customers after each transaction or matter
    closes — not a one-time blast, but a steady stream each month
  - All new Google and Yelp reviews responded to on the client's behalf
    within a timely manner. Responses are written in the client's voice.
    Positive reviews are acknowledged warmly; negative reviews are handled
    professionally and constructively.

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Review velocity — the rate at which new reviews arrive —
  is a key local ranking signal. A business that consistently gains 3–5 new
  reviews per month will compound ahead of competitors who plateau. Review
  responses also signal to Google that the business is active and to prospective
  clients that the business cares about its reputation.

Citation Management
  - Quarterly audit of the client's listings across the top 20–30 local
    directories including: Google, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Yellow Pages,
    Facebook, Foursquare, BBB, and vertical-specific directories
  - NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistencies identified and corrected
  - New citations submitted to high-trust directories where the business
    is missing

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Inconsistent NAP data across directories confuses Google
  and suppresses local rankings. A citation audit ensures the business is
  accurately represented everywhere it appears online.

Website Maintenance (where applicable, content only)
  - Client's website kept current with any changes to business information:
    hours, service offerings, team members, contact details, location info
  - Requires CMS access (Webflow, WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, etc.)
  - Scope: content maintenance only. Does not include design changes,
    new page builds, new features, or structural changes to the site.
    Those are scoped and priced separately.

Keyword Tracking
  - 5 target keywords tracked monthly (practice area + city combinations
    agreed at onboarding, e.g., "immigration lawyer San Francisco")
  - Monthly ranking movement reported — positions tracked, gains and drops
    flagged with context

Reporting & Communication
  - Monthly performance report delivered at the end of each month covering:
    GBP profile views, search impressions, review count and rating trend,
    keyword rank movement for all 5 tracked terms, and a summary of work
    completed that month
  - 30-minute monthly check-in call to walk through the report, discuss
    priorities for the coming month, and flag any issues or opportunities
  - Account communication: marketing-related requests (e.g., update business
    hours, swap a GBP photo, adjust post copy) handled via email within
    48 business hours. This channel covers operational marketing requests
    only — not legal, business, or strategic consulting.

TERMS
-----
  - $750/month
  - Month-to-month, cancel anytime
  - Website maintenance included only where CMS access is granted

TIER 3 — GROWTH
Price: $1,500/month | Recommended from Month 2–3 onwards
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Growth is the content and SEO expansion tier. It is for businesses that have
demonstrated momentum from Month 1 and Starter, and are ready to actively
compete on search — not just maintain their presence but grow it. The focus
shifts from maintenance to visibility expansion: more keywords, more content,
more social presence, and a structured referral strategy.

This is the most common long-term tier for established businesses across all
three LB Marketing verticals.

WHAT'S INCLUDED
---------------

Everything in Starter, plus:

Content Programme — Blog Posts
  - 2 blog posts per month, 500–800 words each
  - Written using AI-assisted drafting with human review and editing for
    accuracy, tone, and vertical relevance
  - Topics are researched and selected based on local keyword opportunity —
    what potential clients are actually searching for in the business's area
  - Examples by vertical:
      Legal:       "What to do if you receive a deportation notice in California"
                   "How long does a green card application take in 2025?"
      Salons:      "Best haircuts for fine hair — what to ask your stylist"
                   "What to expect at your first Balayage appointment"
      Home Svc:    "How to know if your electrical panel needs replacing"
                   "5 signs your plumbing needs attention before winter"
  - Published directly to the client's website where CMS access is available
    and a blog section exists (or can be created). If CMS access is not
    available, content is repurposed across GBP posts and social media instead.
  - Blog content is included where it provides SEO value. For some verticals
    (e.g., salons), social content may be prioritised over blog posts depending
    on what drives the most impact for the client.

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Blog content builds topical authority — Google recognises
  the business as a relevant, expert source for its practice area or service.
  Each post targets long-tail keywords that the homepage doesn't rank for,
  creating additional entry points from search. Over time, a library of
  locally-relevant blog content compounds into a significant ranking and
  traffic advantage.

Social Media
  - 8 posts per month published to the client's primary social platform
  - Platform matched to vertical at onboarding:
      Legal:        LinkedIn (professional audience, thought leadership)
      Salons/Spas:  Instagram (visual content, before/after, promotions)
      Home Services: Facebook and/or Nextdoor (local community, trust)
  - Content mix: approximately 50% repurposed from the month's blog posts
    (key points reformatted for social), 50% original social-first content
    (behind-the-scenes, client outcomes, timely topics, promotions)
  - All graphics designed in Canva using consistent branding per client —
    colours, fonts, and visual style aligned to the business's identity
  - Posting cadence: approximately 2 posts per week

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Social media at this tier is not about vanity metrics —
  it is about staying visible to existing and potential clients in the channels
  they use daily. For a law firm, LinkedIn posts positioning the attorneys as
  knowledgeable and accessible build referral relationships. For a salon,
  Instagram content showing real results drives bookings.

SEO — Expanded Keyword Tracking
  - Keyword tracking expanded to 10–15 terms (up from 5 at Starter)
  - Terms agreed at onboarding or quarterly review — covers primary service
    areas, specific practice areas or service types, and city/neighbourhood
    modifiers (e.g., "immigration lawyer SF", "visa application help San
    Francisco", "DACA lawyer Bay Area")
  - Monthly movement tracked and reported — ranking gains, drops, and new
    keyword opportunities flagged with context and recommended action
  - On-page SEO recommendations provided where keyword gaps are identified —
    title tag updates, meta description improvements, heading structure fixes.
    Implementation handled by LB Marketing where CMS access is available.

Referral Strategy
  - Each month, a list of 3–5 complementary local businesses or referral
    sources is identified — organisations or professionals whose clients
    regularly need what this business offers
  - Examples by vertical:
      Legal:        ESL schools, immigrant community organisations, HR
                    departments at visa-sponsoring companies, notaries
      Salons:       Wedding planners, photographers, nearby hotels,
                    corporate HR for employee perks programmes
      Home Svc:     Real estate agents, property managers, home inspectors,
                    general contractors
  - For each target, a ready-to-send outreach script is drafted — a short,
    personalised message the client can send via email or LinkedIn
  - LB Marketing handles all research and drafting. The client reviews and
    sends the outreach. Execution is the client's responsibility; the strategy
    and scripts are LB Marketing's deliverable.

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Referral relationships are among the highest-ROI client
  acquisition channels for local service businesses. A single real estate
  agent who closes 3 homes per month and refers their buyers to a trusted
  electrician can generate tens of thousands in annual revenue. This tier
  systematises the outreach instead of leaving it to chance.

Reporting Dashboard Access
  - Access to LB Marketing's proprietary client reporting dashboard
  - Shows real-time performance across GBP, reviews, keyword rankings,
    and social engagement in one place
  - STATUS: Coming Q2 2026. Until launch, all reporting is delivered via
    the monthly written report and check-in call.

Reporting & Communication
  - Everything included in Starter reporting, plus:
  - Social engagement metrics included in monthly report (reach, profile
    visits, post interactions)
  - Expanded keyword tracking across 10–15 terms
  - 30-minute monthly strategy call — same format as Starter but agenda
    expands to cover content performance, SEO movement, social results,
    and referral pipeline. Ends with a next-month preview: content planned,
    keywords being targeted, referral targets lined up.
  - Account communication: marketing-related requests via email within
    48 business hours

TERMS
-----
  - $1,500/month
  - Month-to-month, cancel anytime
  - Blog publishing requires CMS access; content repurposed to social/GBP
    where not available
  - Dashboard access launching Q2 2026

TIER 4 — PREMIUM
Price: $2,500/month | Available from Month 4+
Ad spend billed separately — paid directly by client to Google and/or Meta
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Premium is the full-funnel, maximum-presence tier. It is for businesses that
have established a strong organic foundation through earlier tiers and are ready
to layer in paid advertising to accelerate growth. At this level, every channel
is active simultaneously — organic SEO, content, social, paid ads, and
conversion optimisation — and performance is tracked across the entire journey
from first search to closed client.

This tier is best suited for businesses generating consistent revenue and
looking to dominate their local market, not just participate in it.

IMPORTANT: Ad spend is not included in the $2,500/month fee. The client pays
Google and/or Meta directly for their ad budget. LB Marketing manages the
campaigns, strategy, creative, and optimisation. This is the industry standard
model and ensures full transparency — the client always knows exactly how much
is being spent on ads vs. management.

WHAT'S INCLUDED
---------------

Everything in Growth, plus:

Paid Advertising Management
  - Platform selection: Google Ads and/or Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram).
    Platform recommended based on vertical and client goals at onboarding:
      Legal:        Google Search Ads strongly recommended — targets people
                    actively searching for legal help (high intent)
      Salons:       Meta Ads recommended — visual targeting, local audience
                    reach, appointment booking campaigns
      Home Svc:     Google Search Ads for high-intent queries ("emergency
                    plumber near me"); Meta for awareness and retargeting
    Client makes the final decision on which platform(s) to run.
  - Full campaign setup including: campaign structure, ad groups, keyword
    selection (Google) or audience targeting (Meta), ad copywriting, and
    Canva-designed ad creative (images and/or static graphics)
  - Ongoing monthly optimisation: bid strategy adjustments, underperforming
    ads paused, audience segments refined, budget allocation reviewed
  - All campaigns managed within the client's own Google Ads / Meta Business
    Manager account — client retains full ownership and visibility at all times
  - Monthly ad performance summary included in the monthly report: impressions,
    clicks, cost per click, leads generated, and cost per lead

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Organic SEO builds long-term visibility but takes time.
  Paid ads deliver immediate, targeted exposure to people actively searching
  for or interested in the business's services right now. Combined with the
  organic foundation built in earlier tiers, paid ads close the gap between
  visibility and booked clients.

Conversion Rate Optimisation (CRO) — Dedicated Landing Pages
  - A dedicated landing page is built for each paid ad campaign — traffic is
    never sent to the homepage, which is a common and costly mistake
  - Each landing page is purpose-built to convert a specific type of visitor:
    clear headline matching the ad, concise description of the service,
    trust signals (reviews, credentials, years in business), and a prominent
    single CTA (call now, book a consultation, get a free quote)
  - Built in the client's existing CMS (WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, etc.)
    or in Webflow where no existing site is present
  - Pages reviewed and refined quarterly based on performance data —
    if a page has a high click rate but low conversions, it gets updated

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Sending paid ad traffic to a generic homepage is one of
  the most common ways agencies waste client ad spend. A dedicated landing
  page with a single clear action can double or triple conversion rates
  compared to a homepage. This is where the real ROI of paid ads is unlocked.

Link Building
  - Up to 5 link placements per month targeting authoritative local and
    vertical-specific directories and publications
  - Vertical-specific directory targets include:
      Legal:        Avvo, Justia, FindLaw, Lawyers.com, Super Lawyers,
                    Martindale-Hubbell, state bar directories
      Salons/Spas:  StyleSeat, Vagaro, Salon Today, Beauty Launchpad
      Home Svc:     Houzz, Angi (formerly Angie's List), HomeAdvisor,
                    Thumbtack, NARI (for remodelers), NECA (for electricians)
  - Local press outreach: pitching the client for features or mentions in
    local SF publications, neighbourhood blogs, community organisations,
    and local news outlets
  - Note: "Up to 5 placements" reflects realistic capacity — directory
    submissions are more predictable than press placements, which depend
    on editorial interest and timing

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Backlinks from authoritative, relevant sources are one
  of the strongest signals Google uses to determine how trustworthy and
  prominent a local business is. Directory links build baseline authority;
  press placements and local citations build the kind of prominence that
  separates map pack leaders from everyone else.

AI Search Optimisation — AEO (Experimental)
  - Content structured to improve the business's visibility in AI-generated
    answers: Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT responses, Gemini recommendations,
    and Perplexity citations for local queries
  - Targets high-intent conversational queries like:
      "Who is the best immigration lawyer in San Francisco?"
      "What should I do if I get a deportation notice in California?"
      "Which electrician in SF has the best reviews?"
  - Applied to blog content and landing pages — structured with clear
    question-and-answer formatting, authoritative citations, and schema
    markup where applicable
  - IMPORTANT: AEO is an emerging and evolving field. Best practices are
    still being defined as AI search engines mature. This is included as an
    experimental feature — LB Marketing will apply current best practices
    but cannot guarantee specific AI placement outcomes.

  WHY THIS MATTERS: AI-powered search is changing how people find local
  businesses. Appearing in a ChatGPT or Gemini answer to a local legal
  question is increasingly valuable — and most local competitors are not
  thinking about this yet. Early investment in AEO-structured content builds
  an advantage that will compound as AI search grows.

Competitor Monitoring
  - Monthly automated snapshot tracking the top 3–5 local competitors across:
      - Google review count and star rating trend
      - Map pack ranking for shared target keywords
      - GBP activity (posting frequency, new photos, new services)
      - Any significant changes to their online presence
  - Included in the monthly report with a brief commentary on what the data
    means for the client's positioning — is the gap closing, holding, or
    widening?

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Premium clients are investing in market dominance, not
  just maintenance. Knowing how competitors are moving — and whether your
  gains are pulling ahead of or tracking with the market — is essential
  context for strategic decisions.

Quarterly Strategy Document
  - A formal written strategy document produced at the end of each quarter
    (every 3 months)
  - NOT a recap of the past 90 days — this is a forward-looking plan
  - Contents:
      - What is working and why (channels, content types, campaigns)
      - What is being deprioritised or stopped and why
      - Strategic priorities for the next quarter
      - Specific KPI targets for the next 90 days (review count, keyword
        positions, cost per lead, etc.)
      - Any recommended changes to platform, budget, or channel mix
  - Delivered as a written document prior to the quarterly strategy call
    so the client can review it in advance

  WHY THIS MATTERS: Most agencies do a quarterly call. A written document
  forces rigour — every claim has to be defensible on paper, not just
  explained verbally. It also gives the client something to reference,
  share internally, and hold the agency accountable to.

Reporting & Communication
  - Everything in Growth reporting, plus:
  - Ad performance metrics in monthly report (impressions, clicks, CPC,
    leads, cost per lead, ROAS where trackable)
  - Competitor snapshot in monthly report
  - Quarterly strategy document (written, delivered before the quarterly call)
  - 30-minute monthly strategy call with next-month preview
  - Priority account communication: marketing-related requests handled within
    24 business hours (vs. 48 hours at Starter and Growth)

TERMS
-----
  - $2,500/month (management fee only)
  - Ad spend billed separately — client pays Google/Meta directly
  - Month-to-month, cancel anytime
  - CMS access required for CRO landing pages; built in Webflow where
    no existing site is present
